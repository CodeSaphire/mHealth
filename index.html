WAYS TO IMPROVE MENTAL HEALTH

1. Get restful sleep.

Sleep isn't just a nonnegotiable for physical health. It also plays an essential role in mental health. In one study, researchers found that people who averaged 6 hours of sleep or less per night were about 2.5 times more likely to report frequent mental distress than those who averaged more than 6 hours of sleep. The quality of your sleep matters, too. Disrupted sleep can contribute to mental health symptoms.

2. Cut back on social media

Reading a lot of information about other people's lives on various social media websites or apps such as Facebook, Instagram and Twitter may cause someone to compare themselves and develop negative feelings of low self-worth, which increases feelings of anxiety and depression over information that may not necessarily be true.

3. Strengthen your relationships

Humans are social creatures, and strong relationships can have a positive influence on your mental health in various ways such as: ease feelings of loneliness make it easier to get emotional support, add meaning to your life. You have plenty of options for cultivating positive connections and nurturing your friendships such as: Keep in touch by checking in regularly, even with just a quick text or funny meme, meet up for a morning walk or breakfast, call for a short chat during your lunch break, schedule biweekly or monthly dinner dates.

4. Move your body on your terms

Exercise offers a range of mental health benefits, including: relieving stress, lifting mood, helping you fall asleep faster and sleep longer, helping you manage symptoms of depression and anxiety conditions. Movement can involve something different for every person, and it doesn't have to mean going to the gym unless you genuinely want to. Instead, make movement enjoyable for you by opting for physical activities that work best for your body, health, and preferences. Start by experimenting with a range of physical activities and keep doing the ones that resonate with you.

5. Savor nutrient-rich foods

Some foods can affect your mental health. Try including mind-boosting food in your diet. Examples of these foods include: berries, bananas, beans, whole grains, fatty fish, like salmon. It can also help to simply make sure you fuel your body every day by eating. Eating anything is better than eating nothing. Drinking plenty of water throughout the day can also have benefits. Certain foods, namely alcohol, caffeine, refined carbs, and added sugars, may worsen anxiety symptoms. So, limiting these foods can help ease some of your symptoms.

6. Know when to take it easy

On difficult days, you might find it tough to do any of the above, which might make you feel even worse. If so try being more compassionate to yourself and apply strategies like: Organizing your room, setting a timer to clean something for just 5 minutes, buying a prepackaged meal when cooking anything feels close to impossible.Any similar approach you think you can try? Commit to taking one small step every day.
