What is Mental Health ?

Mental Health refers to our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, geel and act. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make healthy choices. Mental health is important at every stage of life.

Mental and physical health are equally important components of overall health. Mental health problems such as depression increase the risk of some physical health problems, like diabetes,heart disease, and stroke. Likewise, the presence of chronic conditions can increase the risk of mental illness.

Can your mental health change over time ?

Yes, it's important to rememeber that a person's mental health can change over time, depending on many factors. When the expectations placed on a person exceed their resources and coping abilities, their mental health could decline. For example, if someone is working long hours, caring for a relative, or experiencng economic hardship, they may experience poor mental health.

Mental health problems are commonly refffrered to as Mental disorders. A mental disorder is characterized by a clinically significant disturbance in an individual's cognition, emotional regulation or behaviour

How common are mental illnesses?

Mental illnesses are among the most common health conditions in the United states,More that 1 in 5 US adults live with a mental illness. Over 1 in 5 youths (age 13-18). About 1 in 25 U.S adults lives with a serious mental illness such as schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, or major depression

What causes mental illness ?

Factors that can lead to mental illness include:-

  • Adverse Childhood experiences, such as trauma or a history of abuse (for example, child abuse, sexual assault, witnessing violence, etc)
  • Experiences related to oter ongoing (chronic) medical conditions, such as cancer or diabetes.
  • Biological factors or chemical imbalances in the brain
  • Use of aclcohol or drugs
  • Having feeling of loneliness or isolation

Mental Health Conditions

Anxiety

Persistent unease, excessive worry, and fear. It can disrupt daily life and relationships, causing phusical and emotional symptoms. Symptoms can be very severe enough to result in significant distress or significant impairment in functioning.

Depression

Overwhelming sadness, loss of interest, and lack of energy. It affects moods, thoughts, and daily functioning. During a depressive eppisode, persorn experiences depressed mood or loss of pleasure or interest in activities, for most of the day, nearly every day, for atleast two weeks.

Bipolar Disorder

A complex mental health condition characterized by extreme shifts in mood, energy and activity levels. It involves episodes of mania, where individuals experience heightened euphoria, and impulsivity, followed by periods of depression.
