Frequently Asked Questions

Mental health refers to a person's emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how people think, feel, and act, and helps determine how individuals handle stress, relate to others, and make choices.

There are several ways to improve your mental health:

  • Engage in regular physical exercise.
  • Practice relaxation techniques such as deep breathing or meditation.
  • Maintain a balanced and nutritious diet.
  • Ensure you get enough quality sleep.
  • Seek support from loved ones and maintain social connections.
  • Consider professional help if needed.

Supporting someone with a mental health condition involves:

  • Being understanding, empathetic, and non-judgmental.
  • Listening actively and offering your presence and support.
  • Encouraging them to seek professional help if needed.
  • Educating yourself about their specific condition to better understand their needs.

Some common mental health disorders include:

  • Anxiety disorders (e.g., generalized anxiety disorder, panic disorder).
  • Depression and depressive disorders.
  • Bipolar disorder.
  • Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD).
  • Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD).
  • Schizophrenia.

If you are experiencing persistent symptoms that significantly impact your daily life, it may be beneficial to seek professional help. Some signs that indicate the need for professional assistance include:

  • Persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, or emptiness.
  • Intense anxiety or panic attacks.
  • Difficulty functioning at work, school, or in relationships
  • Thoughts of self-harm or suicide.
  • Substance abuse or excessive use of alcohol.
  • Significant changes in appetite, sleep patterns, or energy levels.

Remember, reaching out for help is a sign of strength, and mental health professionals are trained to provide support and guidance.
